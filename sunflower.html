Sunflower

Sunflowers are iconic plants known for their large, showy blooms that turn to follow the sun throughout the day, a phenomenon known as heliotropism. Here's a comprehensive guide on sunflowers, including their characteristics, types, planting procedure, and care.

Types of Sunflowers:

  • Giant Sunflowers: These varieties are known for their towering height and large flower heads, making them popular for ornamental purposes and seed production.
  • Dwarf Sunflowers: Compact varieties suitable for smaller gardens or container growing.
  • Decorative Sunflowers: Varieties bred for their unique colors, shapes, or patterns, often used in floral arrangements.
  • Edible Seed Sunflowers: Varieties with large, plump seeds that are commonly harvested for snacking or oil production.

What to do and what not to do

  • Planting Procedure:
    • Spring: Plant sunflower seeds after the last frost date in your area, once the soil has warmed up to around 55°F (13°C).
    • Sunlight: Sunflowers require full sun, meaning they need at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight per day.
    • Soil: Choose well-draining soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5. Sunflowers can tolerate a wide range of soil types but prefer fertile, loamy soil.
